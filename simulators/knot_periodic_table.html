Nuclear Spin Table — J by subshell rows ▼
Why these spin numbers? — Three rules determine J:
Even-even (Z even, N even) → J = 0 always — all nucleon spins pair off.
Odd-A → J is half-integer (1/2, 3/2, 5/2, …) — set by the unpaired nucleon’s subshell j.
Odd-odd (Z odd, N odd) → J is a nonzero integer — two unpaired nucleons couple.
QOU basis: each nucleon is a spin-½ knot; the tensor product (½)⊗A decomposes into VJ via q-Clebsch–Gordan. Jq² = [j]q·[j+1]q is the 𝔾m Casimir.
𝔾m breaking: δ = (Jq² − j(j+1)) / j(j+1) measures the fractional q-deformation of spin. The Σδ% column gives the mean breaking per subshell row. At magic shells (★), the Kummer filtration obstruction vanishes: n | χ(A) where the divisors of |χ| = 6 are {1, 2, 3, 6} (→ μ2, μ3, μ6 torsion levels).
